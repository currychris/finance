GOOD THINGS

Financing offers several advantages, including the ability to make larger purchases without immediate drain on savings and the potential for more flexible payment options. It can also help businesses manage cash flow, improve credit scores, and may offer tax benefits. For Consumers:

Affordability:

Financing allows consumers to purchase items they might not otherwise be able to afford by spreading out the cost over time.

Flexibility:

Financing provides flexibility in payment terms, allowing consumers to choose a repayment schedule that suits their budget. Opportunity Cost: Financing frees up cash that can be used for other purposes, such as investments or emergencies. Potential Credit Improvement: Making timely payments on a financed loan can help improve credit scores.

For Businesses:

Easier on the Budget: Financing can make large purchases more manageable for businesses, especially those with limited cash on hand.

Flexibility in Payment Options:

Businesses can choose from various financing options, such as hire purchase, finance lease, or refinancing.

Opportunity Cost:

Financing allows businesses to use cash for other investments or strategic acquisitions rather than tying it up in a single large purchase.

Tax Benefits:

Interest paid on business loans may be tax-deductible, reducing taxable income and potentially improving profitability.

Fuelling Business Growth:

Financing can enable businesses to invest in growth activities like hiring, marketing, or purchasing equipment.

Build Credit:

Making timely payments on business loans can help improve a business's credit score, according to OnDeck.
